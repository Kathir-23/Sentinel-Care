Staff Advisory: All patient-facing staff must follow full PPE protocol in isolation wards. Refer to Section 4 (Isolation Protocol) below for detailed ward-level requirements.

Prevention Guidelines

Daily protective measures for all staff

  • Wear a 3-ply or N95 mask at all times in clinical areas.
  • Maintain at least 1 metre physical distance from patients and colleagues where possible.
  • Wash hands with soap for at least 20 seconds or use 70%+ alcohol-based sanitiser after each patient contact.
  • Avoid touching eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands.
  • Ensure adequate ventilation in all shared spaces — open windows where safe to do so.
  • Do not attend work if you have fever, cough, or any Covid-19 symptoms — notify the duty manager immediately.

Symptom Reference

Recognise and triage early

Common Onset Symptoms

  • Fever or chills
  • Dry cough
  • Tiredness or fatigue
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat

Less Common / Moderate

  • Aches and body pains
  • Diarrhoea or nausea
  • Conjunctivitis (pink eye)
  • Headache

Serious — Seek Immediate Medical Attention

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain or persistent pressure
  • Loss of speech or movement
  • Confusion or altered consciousness

PPE Protocol

Personal Protective Equipment standards

N95 Mask Isolation wards & ICU
Face Shield During aerosol-generating procedures
Gloves Change between every patient
Gown / Apron Fluid-resistant; dispose after each use
Donning and doffing must follow the hospital's established sequence. Refer to the infection control poster in each ward.

Isolation Protocol

Ward-level containment procedures

  • Confirmed or suspected Covid-19 patients must be admitted to a designated isolation ward immediately.
  • Single-occupancy rooms with negative pressure are preferred for confirmed cases.
  • Restrict movement — patient leaves the room only for essential clinical procedures.
  • Limit the number of staff entering the isolation room; assign a dedicated nursing team where possible.
  • All visitors are prohibited unless approved by the attending physician; visits are limited to 30 minutes with full PPE.
  • Inform the Infection Control Officer within 1 hour of a confirmed diagnosis.
  • Conduct terminal disinfection of the room after patient discharge or transfer.
